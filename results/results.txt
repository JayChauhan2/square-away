**Chain Rule and Applications**

**Problem 4:**
$y = \sqrt[7]{\log(\frac{1}{x})}$

**Solution:**

Applying the chain rule:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{7} \left(\log(\frac{1}{x})\right)^{\frac{1}{7}-1} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \left(\log(\frac{1}{x})\right)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{7} \left(\log(\frac{1}{x})\right)^{-\frac{6}{7}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \left(\log(x^{-1})\right)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{7} \left(\log(\frac{1}{x})\right)^{-\frac{6}{7}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} (-\log x)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{1}{7} \left(\log(\frac{1}{x})\right)^{-\frac{6}{7}} \cdot \frac{1}{x}$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{1}{7x \left(\log(\frac{1}{x})\right)^{\frac{6}{7}}}$

**Problem (Implicit Differentiation):**
$y = \sin^4x + \sin(x^4) + \sin 4x + x^4$

**Solution:**

Applying the chain rule and power rule for differentiation:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(\sin^4x) + \frac{d}{dx}(\sin(x^4)) + \frac{d}{dx}(\sin 4x) + \frac{d}{dx}(x^4)$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4\sin^3x \cdot \frac{d}{dx}(\sin x) + \cos(x^4) \cdot \frac{d}{dx}(x^4) + \cos(4x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx}(4x) + 4x^3$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4\sin^3x \cdot \cos x + \cos(x^4) \cdot (4x^3) + \cos(4x) \cdot 4 + 4x^3$
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4\cos x \sin^3x + 4x^3\cos(x^4) + 4\cos(4x) + 4x^3$
